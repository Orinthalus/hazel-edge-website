Where to go, what to do

Things worth your time

🥾 Walks & wild places

  • Hadrian's Wall Path · 20 min drive
    UNESCO World Heritage Site — walk sections of the Wall with sweeping views north and south.
  • Talkin Tarn Country Park · 20 min drive
    Peaceful lake and woodland walks, great for families and dogs. Café on site.
  • North Pennines AONB · 30 min drive
    England's largest AONB — moorland, waterfalls and wide skies. Cross Fell is the highest point in the Pennines.
  • Solway Coast · 25 min drive
    A quiet, distinctive coastline with vast estuary views, wading birds and a raw, unmanicured feel.
  • Local lanes & footpaths · from the door
    Quiet roads and footpaths start close by — good for morning walks without getting in the car.

🏰 Historic border country

  • Hadrian's Wall · 20 min drive
    Housesteads, Vindolanda and Birdoswald all within reach — some of the best Roman history in Britain.
  • Carlisle Castle · 15 min drive
    A medieval fortress with a long and layered history — well worth an hour of your time.
  • Lanercost Priory · 20 min drive
    Beautiful ruined priory in a peaceful riverside setting. Free to explore the grounds.
  • Naworth Castle · 20 min drive
    Historic fortified house with an interesting history — occasional open days.
  • Scottish Borders · 30 min drive
    Cross the border easily — Langholm, Gretna and Dumfries all within reach for a day out.

🍽️ Food, shops & local life

  • Carlisle · 15 min drive
    A proper city — good market, independent shops, cafés and restaurants. Well worth a proper wander rather than just a supermarket run.
  • Brampton · 15 min drive
    A small market town with independent shops, a good butcher, and a friendly local feel.
  • Farm shops & delis · various
    Several farm shops in the area for local produce, including meat, dairy and seasonal veg.
  • Local pubs · various
    Good traditional pubs within a short drive — we include a guide in your welcome information.

☔ Rainy day ideas

  • Tullie House Museum, Carlisle
    Excellent local history and Roman collections — a genuine find, and free for much of it.
  • Carlisle Cathedral
    One of England's smaller cathedrals but with beautiful stained glass and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Vindolanda Museum · 35 min
    One of the best Roman archaeology sites in Britain — incredible finds, brilliant interpretation.
  • Brampton Bookshop & cafés
    A good excuse to browse and take your time. Good coffee nearby.
  • Lake District galleries & museums · 45 min
    Keswick Museum, Rheged visitor centre and more if the weather closes in properly.
The best kind of day

Slow days at Hazel Edge

A book, the view, a long lunch and nowhere to be. Some of the best days here never leave the garden.

The outdoor bathing area, the wood burner, the herb pots by the door — Hazel Edge is designed for the kind of day where you do less than you planned and feel better for it.
