Sustainability at Hazel Edge

Not perfection or grand claims. Just trying to live more thoughtfully with the land around us — and sharing that process with the guests who stay here.

Our approach

Comfort and a lighter footprint

"Not about perfection or grand claims. It is about trying to live more thoughtfully with the land around us."

Hazel Edge is part of Liddel Lodge, our smallholding on the northern edge of Cumbria. Since arriving here, we have planted native woodland, begun restoring areas of meadow, and planted willow coppice with the long-term aim of growing enough firewood for both Hazel Edge and Liddel Lodge. It is a work in progress, but the aim is simple: to make the land more productive, more biodiverse and more resilient for the future.

We also enjoy sharing this way of life. Guests at Hazel Edge get to experience a little of it during their stay, and we share the wider journey through our YouTube channel, The View From the Clouds.

Hazel Edge is part of an ongoing attempt to live more carefully, grow more of what we need, restore what we can, and enjoy the beauty of this place without taking it for granted.

How we are doing it

The details

🔥

Heating & energy

Hazel Edge is heated using an air source heat pump, keeping the cottage warm and comfortable throughout the year without relying on oil or gas.

We have solar panels at Liddel Lodge, helping us generate renewable electricity on site. In the future we hope to add solar panels to Hazel Edge too. Our electricity is supplied through Octopus Energy on a renewable tariff — so the energy we use is chosen with care.

💧

Spring water

The water at Hazel Edge comes from a spring on the hill. It is one of the quiet luxuries of staying here — fresh, natural water from the landscape itself, not the mains.

Because it is a private spring supply, we ask guests to use water thoughtfully. Not anxiously, just with the awareness that it is something precious. Not leaving taps running unnecessarily, using water sensibly — small things that really do help.

🪵

Firewood & coppice

We have planted willow coppice with the long-term aim of becoming increasingly self-sufficient in firewood for Hazel Edge and our own home.

Coppicing is a traditional way of managing trees. Instead of cutting a tree down once and replanting, the tree is cut and allowed to regrow from the same roots — simple, renewable and well suited to the wetter parts of our land. It also creates habitat, makes use of ground that would otherwise be waterlogged, and gives us a future supply of local firewood. You can follow this project on our YouTube channel.

🌿

Nature restoration

Since arriving at Liddel Lodge, we have planted native woodland species — hazel, birch, rowan, alder and field maple across several areas. Meadow restoration is underway in others: less cutting, no fertiliser, more time.

This is patient work. Trees grow slowly, meadows take longer. But year by year the landscape becomes more sheltered, more varied and more alive — and guests at Hazel Edge are staying somewhere genuinely connected to that ongoing process.

🧴

Thoughtful details inside

Inside Hazel Edge, we use eco-conscious products wherever practical: refillable toiletries, eco cleaning products, Fairtrade tea, coffee and hot chocolate, soft eco toilet paper, and a toilet paper gel alternative to disposable wipes.

Because Hazel Edge is on a septic tank, we ask guests to flush only toilet paper and put everything else in the bin provided. It is a simple but important part of caring for the land and water around us.

🥦

Herbs, vegetables & seasonal produce

We grow a lot of our own vegetables at Liddel Lodge, and when the garden is generous, we love being able to share seasonal produce with guests.

Outside Hazel Edge, we keep herbs in pots — rosemary, thyme, mint and parsley — for guests to pick a little fresh flavour for their meals. It is a small touch, but one that adds to the feeling of being somewhere rooted, relaxed and real.

🪑

Second-hand & upcycled furnishings

Where possible, we choose reused, upcycled or second-hand furnishings rather than buying everything new. Not everything has to be brand new to be beautiful — older pieces often bring more character, less waste and a better story.

This is not a rigid rule, but it is a preference we apply wherever it makes sense, and one that gives Hazel Edge a feel that is more personal and less generic.

Still growing
